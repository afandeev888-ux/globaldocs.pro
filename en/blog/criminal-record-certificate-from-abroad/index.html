How to Get a Criminal Record Certificate from Abroad: 4 Methods

April 30, 2025 12 min read

A criminal record certificate is one of the most requested documents for Russians living abroad. It's required for visas, employment, citizenship applications, adoption, and many other situations. This article covers all methods to obtain the certificate from abroad, timelines, costs, and common mistakes to avoid.

What is a Criminal Record Certificate and Why You Need It

A certificate of presence (absence) of criminal record is an official document issued by the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Russian Federation (MVD). It contains information about whether the citizen has an unexpunged or outstanding criminal record, as well as facts of criminal prosecution on the territory of the Russian Federation. The document is issued on an official form with the signature of an authorized official and the MVD seal.

Abroad, this document is required in many different situations. It is impossible to do without it when applying for citizenship or residence permit in most countries of the world. Employers abroad, especially in education, medicine, finance, and work with children, routinely request this certificate as part of the background check procedure.

When adopting a child or arranging guardianship, a criminal record certificate is a mandatory document in the vast majority of jurisdictions. Additionally, it may be needed to obtain licenses for certain types of activities, open bank accounts, participate in tenders, and even for admission to some foreign educational institutions.

Four Methods to Obtain the Certificate from Abroad

Today there are four main methods to obtain a criminal record certificate if you are outside of Russia. Each has its advantages, disadvantages, and limitations. The choice of the optimal method depends on your timeframe, budget, availability of a confirmed account on Gosuslugi, and access to consular institutions.

Method 1: Through Gosuslugi Portal (Online)

This is the fastest and most convenient method for those who have a confirmed account on the Russian public services portal. The application is submitted completely online, without the need to visit any institutions.

  • Log in to gosuslugi.ru with a confirmed account
  • Find the service "Obtaining a certificate of presence (absence) of criminal record"
  • Fill out the application: full name, date and place of birth, passport details, registration address
  • Choose the method of receipt: electronic document (PDF with MVD digital signature) or paper original
  • Submit the application and wait for a notification of readiness

Timeline: 7-30 days. Cost: free.

Method 2: Through the Russian Consulate

Russian consular institutions abroad can accept an application for a criminal record certificate and send it to the MVD of Russia through diplomatic channels. This method is suitable for those who do not have access to Gosuslugi.

The procedure is as follows: you personally visit the consular department of the Russian embassy or consulate, fill out an application, present your passport, and the consulate sends the request to Russia. After the certificate is produced, the MVD sends it back to the consulate via diplomatic mail.

Timeline: 2-4 months. Cost: free or nominal fee. Required: personal visit with Russian passport.

Method 3: By Power of Attorney Through a Representative

One of the most common methods among Russians abroad. You execute a notarized power of attorney to a person in Russia who submits the application to the MVD on your behalf and receives the ready certificate.

Procedure for executing a power of attorney from abroad: contact the consular department of the Russian embassy for notarization of the power of attorney. Specify the powers: right to submit application, right to receive the document, right to obtain apostille. Send the original power of attorney to Russia by courier.

Timeline: 1-2 months (including mailing). Cost: consular fee + courier delivery.

Apostille on the Criminal Record Certificate

If you plan to use the criminal record certificate abroad, in the vast majority of cases you will need to obtain an apostille. An apostille is a special stamp certifying the authenticity of a document for its recognition in countries that are parties to the 1961 Hague Convention. Without an apostille, the certificate will not have legal force outside of Russia.

Procedure for Obtaining an Apostille

To obtain an apostille, you must provide the original criminal record certificate to the Ministry of Justice of the Russian Federation. Documents can be submitted in person, through a representative by power of attorney, or through a specialized service.

  • State fee: 2,500 rubles per document
  • Standard timeline: 3-7 business days
  • Expedited timeline: 1-2 business days (with additional fee)

Electronic Apostille (e-Apostille)

Since 2023, Russia has been implementing an electronic apostille system. Its main advantages are speed of processing and the ability to verify remotely through an online registry. However, e-Apostille is not yet accepted by all countries. For most official procedures, a paper original with a traditional apostille stamp is still required.

Important: An apostille is placed only on the original document. It cannot be placed on a photocopy or notarized copy of the certificate. If you need multiple copies — order each separately.

Certificate Validity Period

The question of the validity period of a criminal record certificate is one of the most frequent and at the same time ambiguous. Formally, according to Russian legislation, the certificate does not have an established validity period. However, in practice, the validity is determined by the requirements of the organization where you submit the document.

Validity Period Where Required
3 months Most common standard. Most EU countries, Germany, Italy, Spain, USA, Canada
1 month Some UAE, South Korea authorities — especially strict requirements
6 months Some CIS countries, certain types of licenses

We recommend ordering the certificate 2-4 weeks before the planned submission, having clarified the validity period required by the receiving party.

Country-Specific Requirements

Requirements for criminal record certificates differ significantly depending on the country where you plan to use it. Below is information for the most popular destinations.

🇩🇪 Germany

  • Certificate with apostille + sworn translation (beeidigte Übersetzung)
  • Translator must have state accreditation in Germany
  • Validity: 3 months
  • Required for residence permit, citizenship, work visa

🇮🇹 Italy

  • Apostille + sworn translation (traduzione giurata)
  • Translator registered with the court in Italy, or legalization at the consulate
  • Validity: 3 months

🇮🇱 Israel

  • Hague Convention participant — apostille is sufficient
  • Translation into Hebrew by a notarized translator in Israel
  • Validity: up to 6 months (sometimes 3 months)

🇺🇸 USA

  • Apostille + notarized translation into English
  • Certified translator is sufficient
  • Validity: 3 months
  • Required for immigration visas, Green Card, government employment

🇦🇪 UAE and Gulf Countries

  • NOT a Hague Convention participant — consular legalization required
  • Process: notary → Ministry of Justice → Ministry of Foreign Affairs → UAE consulate
  • Timeline: 2-4 weeks
  • Similar requirements for Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Over years of working with documents for clients from more than 90 countries, we have encountered hundreds of situations where mistakes led to loss of time and money. Here are the most common ones.

Mistake 1: Ordering the certificate too early or too late

Many order the certificate "in advance", and by the time of submission it has expired. Others wait until the last moment and don't have time. We recommend: order 2-4 weeks before submission, having clarified the validity period with the receiving party.

Mistake 2: Missing apostille

One of the most common mistakes — obtaining the certificate without an apostille. Without it, the document has no legal force abroad. If you order through Gosuslugi or the consulate — the apostille must be obtained separately.

Mistake 3: Wrong type of translation

Not any translation is suitable. For Germany, a sworn translator (beeidigt) is needed, for Italy — one registered with the court, for Israel — a notarized translator in the country. A regular notarized translation in Russia may not be suitable.

Mistake 4: Errors in personal data

Even a small typo in the name, date of birth, or passport number makes the document invalid. Be sure to check all data in the ready certificate before obtaining the apostille.

Mistake 5: Attempting to place an apostille on a copy

An apostille is placed only on the original document. If you need a notarized copy with an apostille — the apostille is placed on the original, then a copy is made. Placing an apostille on a photocopy is impossible by law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get the certificate completely online?

Yes, through the Gosuslugi portal you can receive an electronic document (PDF with MVD digital signature). However, for most official purposes abroad, a paper original with an apostille is required, which cannot be obtained completely online.

Do I need a power of attorney to obtain the certificate?

A power of attorney is not required to obtain a criminal record certificate. This document can be ordered with a passport copy. A power of attorney is only needed to obtain documents from ZAGS (birth, marriage certificates, etc.).

How long is the apostille valid on the certificate?

The apostille has no expiration date — it is valid indefinitely as long as the document itself is valid. However, the validity of the criminal record certificate is determined by the receiving party (usually 3 months). When the certificate is "expired", the apostille on it also stops being accepted.

Can I get an apostille on an electronic certificate?

An electronic apostille (e-Apostille) can be placed on an electronic document (PDF with digital signature). However, not all countries accept this format. For most situations, we recommend a paper original with a traditional apostille stamp.

What if I don't have a confirmed Gosuslugi account?

Without a confirmed account, it is impossible to submit an application online. In this case, you can contact the Russian consulate, execute a power of attorney to a representative in Russia, or use the services of a specialized company (such as GlobalDocs Pro).
