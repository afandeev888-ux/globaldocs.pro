How to Get Documents from Russia to Germany

Germany is one of the most popular destination countries for Russian speakers — whether you are moving for work, family reunification, study or permanent residency. In almost all cases, German authorities will require official documents issued in Russia, and these documents must be properly legalized and translated before they can be accepted.

The process of getting Russian documents recognized in Germany involves several steps: obtaining the original document from the Russian registry (ZAGS), getting an apostille, translating the document into German by a sworn translator, and in some cases obtaining additional certifications. Each of these steps can be time-consuming and complicated, especially if you are no longer in Russia. That is where Global Docs Pro comes in — we handle the entire process from start to finish, so you can focus on your relocation plans.

Since 2015, we have helped thousands of clients obtain and legalize their Russian documents for use in Germany. Our office in Moscow has direct connections with all relevant authorities, consulates and embassies, which allows us to process your requests quickly and efficiently.

💡 Good news: Both Russia and Germany are members of the 1961 Hague Apostille Convention. This means that an apostille is sufficient for legalization of most Russian documents in Germany — consular legalization is not required.

Apostille for German Authorities

The apostille is a standardized certification that confirms the authenticity of a public document. When you present a Russian document to a German authority (Ausländerbehörde, Standesamt, Bürgeramt, university, employer), the document must bear an apostille stamp from the competent Russian authority. Without an apostille, your document will not be accepted.

In Russia, apostilles are issued by different authorities depending on the document type. Civil registry documents (birth, marriage, divorce certificates) receive apostilles from the regional ZAGS authorities. Educational documents (diplomas, transcripts) are apostilled by the regional education departments. Court documents and powers of attorney receive apostilles from regional courts. Our team knows exactly which authority to approach for each document type, which saves time and avoids rejections.

The processing time for an apostille in Russia depends on the type of document, the issuing region and the competent authority. Some documents are processed quickly, while others may take longer due to the specific requirements of the issuing authority. Contact us and we will provide you with an individual assessment of the expected processing time for your specific case. Every apostille we obtain is verified for accuracy before we send it to you.

Translation into German

German authorities almost always require that your Russian documents be accompanied by a certified translation into German. This translation must be performed by a sworn translator (vereidigte/r Übersetzerin) who is recognized by German courts. A regular translation, even if it is accurate, will not be accepted by the authorities.

We work with experienced Russian-German translator teams who specialize in legal and official document translations. Our translations include a translator's certification statement (Übersetzererkärung) and are formatted to meet the requirements of German authorities. Each translated document is checked by a second translator for accuracy and completeness before delivery.

Common documents that require German translation include: birth certificates, marriage certificates, divorce decrees, diplomas, academic transcripts, police clearance certificates, employment records, medical certificates and powers of attorney.

⚡ Important: For the recognition of professional qualifications (Anerkennungsverfahren), the German authorities typically require a special form of certified translation. We know the specific requirements for each German federal state and ensure your translations meet them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about getting Russian documents for Germany.

Do I need an apostille or consular legalization for documents from Russia to Germany?▼
Since both Russia and Germany are members of the 1961 Hague Apostille Convention, an apostille is sufficient for most documents. Consular legalization is generally not required. However, some specific documents (such as certain commercial documents) may have different requirements. Contact us and we will advise you on your specific case.
How long does it take to get a Russian birth certificate with apostille for Germany?▼
The processing time depends on the type of document, the region in Russia where it was issued, and the current workload of the competent authority. We always provide an individual estimate after reviewing your request. Contact us and we will assess the expected timeline for your specific case.
Does the German translation need to be done by a sworn translator?▼
Yes. German authorities (Ausländerbehörde, Standesamt, etc.) require translations by a sworn translator (vereidigte/r Übersetzerin). Regular translations, even if accurate, will not be accepted. We work with sworn Russian-German translators to ensure your translations are recognized.
Can you help with the recognition of my Russian diploma in Germany (Anerkennung)?▼
We can prepare all the required documents for your Anerkennungsverfahren — apostilled copies of your diploma and transcripts, certified translations into German, and any supplementary documents needed. However, the actual recognition application must be submitted by you directly to the responsible authority in your German federal state.
Do I need to come to Russia in person to get my documents?▼
In most cases, no. We can obtain documents on your behalf using a power of attorney. For standard civil registry documents (birth, marriage certificates), a notarized power of attorney is typically sufficient. Contact us and we will explain the exact requirements for your specific documents.
