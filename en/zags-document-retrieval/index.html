What Is ZAGS?

ZAGS (Civil Registry Office) is the Russian government agency responsible for recording vital life events: births, marriages, divorces, deaths, and name changes. These records are kept at local ZAGS offices throughout Russia, and each event is documented with an official certificate. If you were born, married, divorced, or had a family member pass away in Russia, there is a ZAGS record of it.

For Russian citizens living abroad, obtaining these documents can be challenging. You may need a birth certificate for immigration, a marriage certificate for foreign authorities, or a death certificate of a relative for inheritance purposes. Previously, obtaining these documents required a personal visit to Russia — but not anymore. Global Docs Pro can retrieve any ZAGS document on your behalf without requiring your physical presence in Russia.

📋 Did you know? ZAGS offices in Russia have been maintaining civil records since 1917. Older records (pre-revolutionary) are kept in church archives. We work with both modern ZAGS offices and historical archives to find the documents you need.

Which Documents Can We Obtain?

We retrieve all types of ZAGS documents from any region of Russia. Below is a complete list of document types we handle.

Birth Certificates

Standard birth certificates and duplicate copies. Form 4 extracts for apostille purposes. Includes records from all regions of Russia and former USSR republics.

Marriage Certificates

Marriage certificates and extracts from marriage records. Form 5 and Form 6 certificates. Records from all ZAGS offices in Russia.

Divorce Certificates

Certificates of divorce from ZAGS and court divorce decrees. We can also obtain extracts from divorce records.

Death Certificates

Death certificates for inheritance, pension claims, and other purposes. Available for deaths registered anywhere in Russia.

Name Change Certificates

Certificates of name change, surname change, or patronymic change. Required for proving identity changes in foreign authorities.

Archival Extracts

Historical records and archival extracts from ZAGS. For genealogy research, confirming family relationships, or replacing lost documents.

How We Retrieve ZAGS Documents

1. Send Your Request

We submit the request to the appropriate ZAGS office, follow up on the processing, and collect the document. If the record is archived, we locate it through the regional archive.

Once obtained, we send you photos of the document for confirmation. We can then attach an apostille, arrange translation, and ship the original to your address anywhere in the world via insured courier.

What If I Don't Know Where the Document Was Issued?

Many of our clients don't have complete information about their ZAGS records — they may not know the exact date of an event, the specific ZAGS office, or even the exact names as recorded in Russian documents. This is completely normal and we deal with such cases regularly.

We have experience working with ZAGS archives throughout Russia and can help locate records based on partial information. Provide us with whatever details you have: approximate year, region, names of parties involved, and we'll conduct a search. Archive search services are available for older records that may have been transferred from local ZAGS offices to regional archives.

Frequently Asked Questions About ZAGS Documents

Common questions about retrieving civil registry documents from Russia.

Do I need to come to Russia in person to get a ZAGS certificate?▼
No, you don't need to travel to Russia. We can obtain ZAGS documents on your behalf using a notarized power of attorney. We handle the entire process remotely and deliver the document to your address anywhere in the world.
What documents can I get from ZAGS?▼
We can obtain: birth certificates, marriage certificates, divorce certificates, death certificates, name change certificates, archival extracts from ZAGS records, and duplicate certificates if the original was lost or damaged.
How long does it take to get a ZAGS document?▼
Processing time depends on the region where the event was registered and the type of document. Standard processing typically takes 1-2 weeks. Express processing may be available in some regions. We provide an individual timeline estimate after reviewing your request.
What if I don't know where the document was issued?▼
If you're unsure about the exact ZAGS office, we can help locate the record. We work with archives and can search for records across Russia. Provide us with any information you have — approximate date, region, names — and we'll find the document.
Can I get a ZAGS document for someone else?▼
Yes, you can obtain ZAGS documents for close relatives (parents, children, siblings, spouse) with appropriate documentation. For other persons, additional legal grounds may be required. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.
Do I need an apostille on the ZAGS certificate?▼
If you plan to use the document abroad, you will most likely need an apostille. We provide apostille services in addition to document retrieval. Contact us and we'll advise you on the requirements for your destination country.
