What Is a Police Clearance Certificate

A police clearance certificate (official name: certificate of the presence or absence of a criminal record and/or the fact of criminal prosecution or the termination of criminal prosecution) is an official document issued by the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Russian Federation confirming that no criminal cases have been initiated against a specific individual on the territory of Russia, or containing information about existing criminal records and prosecutions.

This document is one of the most frequently required when moving abroad. Virtually every country in the world requires a police clearance certificate for issuing a work visa, residence permit, permanent residency authorization, employment in certain sectors (education, healthcare, work with children), obtaining licenses and security clearances, as well as for acquiring citizenship or repatriation.

The police clearance certificate is issued by the Information Center (IC) of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Russia at the applicant's place of registration or residence. For individuals located outside Russia, the process of obtaining the certificate can be challenging — it requires a personal visit to the Ministry of Internal Affairs, notarization of a power of attorney for a representative, or submission of an application through the Gosuslugi portal (which requires a verified account and a Russian mobile phone number).

Who Needs a Police Clearance Certificate

A police clearance certificate is required in a wide range of situations, both for moving abroad and within Russia. Below are the most common cases where this document is needed, organized by country and purpose.

🇩🇪

Residence Permit in Germany

The Ausländerbehörde requires the certificate for residence permits, work visas, Blue Card and family reunification visas. The document must bear an apostille and be translated by a sworn translator.

🇪🇸

Residence in Spain

Required for most types of residencia: golden visa, nomada digital, family reunification, work visa. Apostille plus translation by a sworn translator (traductor jurado) into Spanish.

🇮🇱

Repatriation to Israel

The Israeli consulate requires a police clearance certificate for repatriation (aliya) processing. The document must bear an apostille with translation into Hebrew or English.

🇺🇸

Immigration to the USA

Required for green cards, work visas (H-1B), fiance/fiancee visas (K-1) and naturalization. Apostille plus translation; in some cases, a special FBI form is also needed.

🇦🇪

Work and Residence in the UAE

Required for work visas and professional licenses in the UAE. Consular legalization applies (the UAE is not a party to the Hague Convention), plus Arabic translation.

🇨🇦

Immigration to Canada

Necessary for Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Program, work visas and study permits. Consular legalization or apostille — depends on the specific program.

🇬🇧

Visas and Residence in the UK

Required for work visas (Skilled Worker), student visas, family reunification visas and indefinite leave to remain. Apostille plus notarized translation.

🇨🇳

Work Visa for China

Chinese authorities require the certificate for a work visa (Z-visa) and work permits. Consular legalization through the Ministry of Justice, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Chinese consulate.

💼

Employment and Licensing

In many countries the certificate is mandatory for work in education, healthcare, finance, as well as for obtaining licenses, security clearances and working with children.

Apostille or Consular Legalization — What Your Certificate Needs

Like any official document from Russia, a police clearance certificate requires legalization for use abroad. The type of legalization depends on the country where you will present the certificate.

For **Hague Convention countries** (Germany, Spain, Italy, France, Israel, the USA, the United Kingdom, Japan, Australia and over 120 other states), an **apostille** is sufficient. The apostille on a police clearance certificate is placed by the regional authority of the Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs — this is a standard procedure that we perform regularly.

For **countries that have not signed the Hague Convention** (the UAE, China, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Canada and others), **consular legalization** is required — a multi-step process through the Ministry of Justice, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the consulate of the destination country. This process takes longer and costs more, but we have extensive experience completing it for all countries on this list.

⚡ Important: A police clearance certificate has a limited validity period. In most countries it is accepted within **3-6 months** from the date of issue. Keep this in mind when planning — it is better to order the certificate closer to the date you submit documents to the authority of the destination country.

What Information Does the Certificate Contain

The police clearance certificate contains the following information:

  • Last name, first name and patronymic of the applicant (exactly matching the passport);
  • Date and place of birth;
  • Passport series and number, date of issue and the authority that issued the document;
  • Citizenship;
  • Information on the presence or absence of criminal records in the Russian Federation;
  • Information on the presence or absence of criminal prosecution;
  • Information on the termination of criminal prosecution (if applicable);
  • Date of issue of the certificate and the name of the issuing authority (IC of the Ministry of Internal Affairs).

If no information about criminal records or prosecution exists for the individual, the certificate contains the following wording: "No records of criminal record (or the fact of criminal prosecution or the termination of criminal prosecution) exist for [Full Name]."

The police clearance certificate itself does not have a formal expiry date — it simply confirms your status at the time of issue.
